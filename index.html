Days ashore in Andalucía

From your calendar: five days based in Málaga, radiating out to Granada and Ronda. Short overviews of each stop, plus a few things worth adding.

Sun · Nov 29 Land at Málaga 12:15 PM, taxi to Palacio Solecio (~20 min), settle in. An easy first evening — dinner in the Old Town, a walk down Calle Larios under the Christmas lights.
Mon · Nov 30

Málaga on foot

Everything today is walkable from the hotel — a compact loop through Moorish, Roman and Picasso-era Málaga, ending at the waterfront.

  • Alcazaba11th-century Moorish fortress-palace rising above the center — layered gardens, courtyards and horseshoe arches, one of the best-preserved in Spain.
  • Roman Theatre1st-century-BC theatre at the foot of the Alcazaba, rediscovered in 1951. Free to view; floodlit and photogenic at dusk.
  • Málaga CathedralNicknamed "La Manquita" (the one-armed lady) for its unfinished second tower. The rooftop tour is the highlight — views over the city and port.
  • Gibralfaro CastleThe hilltop Moorish castle above the Alcazaba, linked by ramparts. The climb (or a short taxi) earns the best panorama of the harbor and bullring.
  • Picasso Museum200+ works in the Renaissance Buenavista Palace, a few minutes from Picasso's birthplace on Plaza de la Merced.
  • Plaza de la Constitución & Calle LariosThe main square and Málaga's elegant marble shopping street — especially lovely in early December with the light displays.
  • Muelle UnoThe revamped port promenade with the Pompidou "cube," waterfront dining and an easy sunset stroll back toward La Malagueta beach.
Worth adding
  • Early December means the Calle Larios Christmas-lights show — usually a nightly music-and-light spectacle. Time an evening around it.
  • Buy the combined Alcazaba + Gibralfaro ticket and do them as one uphill route to save doubling back.
  • Lunch at Mercado de Atarazanas (market tapas + vermouth), and try espetos — sardines skewered and grilled over driftwood — at a beach chiringuito in La Malagueta or Pedregalejo.
Tue · Dec 1

Granada & the Alhambra

Private car from the hotel. Your Alhambra admission is 11:00 (be on site by 10:00) — that time is the strict Nasrid Palaces entry slot. Order ref 1401520mmnzz.

  • The AlhambraThe crown jewel of Moorish Spain: the Nasrid Palaces with their filigree stucco and the Court of the Lions, the Generalife gardens, and the Alcazaba fortress — all set against the Sierra Nevada.
  • Albaicín & Mirador de San NicolásThe whitewashed old Moorish quarter, with the famous viewpoint framing the Alhambra against the mountains — best near sunset.
  • Cathedral & Royal ChapelIf time allows down in the center: the Renaissance cathedral and the tombs of Ferdinand and Isabella next door.
Good to know
  • Granada is ~1.5 hrs each way. Ask your driver to hold late-afternoon time for the Albaicín and Mirador de San Nicolás so you catch the Alhambra glowing at sunset.
  • The timed Nasrid Palace entry is unforgiving — miss the window and it's forfeited. Wear comfortable shoes; the grounds are large and hilly.
  • Granada still honors the free-tapa-with-every-drink tradition — an easy, cheap lunch between sights.
Wed · Dec 2

Ronda

One of Andalucía's most dramatic towns, split in two by a 120-meter gorge. About 1.5–2 hrs from Málaga through scenic mountain country.

  • Puente NuevoThe iconic 18th-century "New Bridge" spanning the El Tajo gorge — Ronda's signature image, connecting the old and new towns.
  • Mirador de AldehuelaThe viewpoint beside the bridge with the best head-on look down the gorge — the classic photo spot.
  • Casa Don BoscoA genteel early-20th-century house with tiled terraces and gardens perched right on the cliff edge.
  • Alameda del TajoA leafy cliff-top promenade and park with balconies that hang out over the valley.
  • Old Town (La Ciudad)The Moorish quarter across the bridge — Plaza Duquesa de Parcent, the Mondragón Palace, and quiet cobbled lanes.
Worth adding
  • Ronda's Plaza de Toros is one of the oldest and most storied bullrings in Spain, with a small museum — striking even just architecturally.
  • You're in wine country (D.O. Sierras de Málaga). Book a lunch at a restaurant with a terrace over El Tajo, or a short vineyard tasting nearby.
